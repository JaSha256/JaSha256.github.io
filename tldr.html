TLDR: Age Verification via EUDI Wallet

One-page summary of the full analysis

← Full paper • March 2026

The Proposal

SPD politicians propose mandatory age verification for all social media users — including adults — via the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI). Under-14s would be banned entirely; 14–15-year-olds would get a restricted “youth version.” The EUDI Wallet is EU-mandated infrastructure (eIDAS 2.0) that will exist regardless — the question is whether it should become a precondition for accessing public digital spaces.

The Technical Problem

The EUDI Wallet architecture (ARF v2.8) uses SD-JWT and mdoc credentials with static issuer signatures. The signature is identical each time a credential is presented — any two platforms can trivially correlate users across services. This is not a bug; it is a deliberate design choice. Technology for anonymous credentials has existed for 20+ years (BBS signatures, CL signatures). 16 internationally renowned cryptographers published a joint statement (June 2024) documenting this; the recommendation remains unimplemented.

The ARF’s mitigation — batch issuance (use each credential copy once) — is acknowledged by the ARF itself as a partial solution. Presentations are bounded by batch size, re-issuance creates new tracking vectors, and revocation status checks can re-correlate users despite mitigations (VCR_17/18).

Pseudonyms are now generated locally on the device (improved from earlier versions), but they do not mask the static credential signature — they provide relationship pseudonymity without transaction unlinkability.

The Proportionality Test (Art. 52(1) EU Charter)

Legitimate Aim — Conceded

Child protection is a Treaty obligation. The harms are real: 275,652 confirmed CSAM URLs in 2023 (IWF); NCMEC received 63 million files in 2024; sextortion tripled 2021–2023.

Suitability — Conceded with Reservations

South Korea’s real-name policy (2004–2012) achieved a 0.9 percentage point decline in malicious comments — within measurement error. Australia’s social media ban (enforced December 2025) led to 4.7M accounts removed, but platforms responded through account deletion rather than age verification technology. A constitutional challenge is pending before the High Court.

Necessity — Fails

Less invasive alternatives exist and work:

  • UK Age Appropriate Design Code: 91 significant design changes on major platforms — without any identification infrastructure. Instagram set under-16 accounts to private; TikTok stopped nighttime push notifications; YouTube disabled autoplay for children.
  • EDPB Statement 1/2025: Recommends age assurance (reasonable confidence about age group) over age verification (precise identity) for general services.
  • France CNIL “double anonymity” model: Architectural role separation ensuring no party holds both identity and usage data. ARCOM enforced a compliant standard by August 2025.
  • DSA (already in force): Prohibits profile-based advertising for known minors (Art. 28(2)), mandates algorithmic transparency (Art. 27), bans dark patterns (Art. 25). The problem is an enforcement deficit, not a regulatory gap.

Proportionality Stricto Sensu — Fails

The costs exceed the marginal benefit:

  • Mass infrastructure: Permanent identification system for all users to protect a subset (minors), with no sunset clause.
  • Chilling effects: Wikipedia traffic to “sensitive” articles dropped 27% post-Snowden (Penney, 2016). Surveillance awareness suppresses minority opinion expression.
  • Function creep: The EU CSAM Regulation (“Chat Control”) expanded from CSAM detection to AI-based grooming surveillance of private messages within a single legislative proposal. Mandatory E2EE scanning was later dropped (November 2025) — but the infrastructure precedent persists.
  • eIDAS contradiction: Art. 5a(1): Wallet use “shall be voluntary.” Art. 5a(16): Wallet “shall not be a precondition for accessing” private services. Age verification for social media would violate both.

The Root Cause

The surveillance advertising business model — not user identity — drives platform harms. Engagement algorithms amplify anger (+0.47 SD), anxiety (+0.23 SD) while users prefer chronological feeds (Milli et al., 2025). Meta generates 97.3% of revenue from ads. 54–96% of users reject tracking when given the choice (Apple ATT). Age verification addresses who accesses the platform without changing what the platform does.

The Position

This analysis is pro-better-technology, not anti-technology. It advocates:

  1. Privacy-preserving age assurance (ZKP-based, not identity-linked) where legally required
  2. Design regulation addressing root causes (algorithmic amplification, addictive design)
  3. DSA enforcement (binding researcher access timelines, dedicated budgets, sanctions for non-compliance)
  4. Digital Fairness Act (expected Q3 2026) targeting manipulative design and special protection for minors

Regulating who accesses a platform without simultaneously addressing what the platform does misallocates regulatory priority.
